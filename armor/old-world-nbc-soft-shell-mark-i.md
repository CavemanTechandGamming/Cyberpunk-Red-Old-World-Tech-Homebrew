## Long description

**Mark I NBC soft-shells** layered **filter fabric** and **ballistic scrim** in one **continuous** cut: **hood**, **shoulders**, **torso**—**no gap** at the neck because the Old World still cared about **nerve agents** and **aerosolized junk**. Against **fragments** and **light pistol** threats it **helps**; against **rifles** it is **hope** and **distance**. Seals **dry-rot**; **zippers** **jam**; the **visor** **fogs** if the **exhale valve** fails.

Salvage suits often **lost** their **certified filter cartridges**—replaced with **scavenger charcoal** or **nothing**. Wearing one **closed** in a fight is **hot** and **loud** inside your own breath. **Legs** are **not** part of the shell—use **trousers** or **greaves** separately.

**Appearance:** **Waxy grey, mustard, or dull orange** depending on **agency** paint; **faded stencils** read **BIOHAZARD** or **EVAC ROUTE** in **fonts** that **look** official until you **read** the **typos** from **post-Collapse** stencil jobs. **Hood** **balloons** slightly—**wire** in the **brim** keeps the **visor** **out** from your nose. **Visor** is **wide**, **single pane**, **anti-fog** coating **gone** in **streaks**. **Torso** **wrinkles** like a **raincoat** but **thicker**; **seams** run **under the arms** and **down the spine**. **Gloves** **attach** at **cuff** with **twist-lock** rings—**often missing**. **Boots** **not included**—**elastic** at the **ankle** **rides** over **street shoes**.
