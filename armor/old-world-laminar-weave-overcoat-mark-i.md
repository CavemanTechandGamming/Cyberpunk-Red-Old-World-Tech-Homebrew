# Old World Laminar-Weave Overcoat — Mark I

**Short description:** A pre-Collapse outer layer whose fibers **stiffen under kinetic stress**—early salvage–tier laminar weave, hungry for maintenance, still better than most Night City junk.

**Armor:** Body

**Stopping power — Current:** 7  
**Stopping power — Max:** 7

**Armor penalty:** 0

## Long description

Before the Collapse, corporate security and mid-tier military buyers wore **laminar weave**: flexible fabric that **phase-aligns** on impact, spreading force across the whole panel instead of letting a round punch a clean hole. **Mark I** units are the common salvage pattern: older cells in the weave, more **cold spots**, and alignment that drifts if you do not baby the panels.

Salvaged units are never factory-fresh. Micro-fractures in the alignment mesh mean places the weave wakes up late. Techs who know Old World textiles can **re-tension** the panels; everyone else learns to favor cover anyway. Under extreme stress the weave can **overlock** briefly—panels stay rigid when you need them soft—until someone with the right tools recalibrates the alignment mesh outside a fight.

This is **not** powered hardshell. It does not run an Agent. It is armor you **wear**, maintain, and eventually retire when the weave gives up. **Head** and **legs** are not covered unless you layer other gear.

**Appearance:** A **long, heavy coat** that reads as upscale street or corporate travel—**matte charcoal, deep navy, or faded olive** depending on what batch left the vault. The weave has a **faint rib** in the light when it catches; seams run **vertical along the torso** and **along the outer arms** where the panels join. Collar stands **tall enough** to bury the chin; cuffs are **wide**, sized to clear gloves or light cyber-hands. Hardware is **minimal**—magnetic snaps and low-profile zippers, no obvious Militech badges left intact. Weight sits **shoulders and upper back**; when the weave **snaps** on impact, the fabric goes **rigid along those ribs** for a heartbeat, then relaxes. Worn and dirty examples show **whitening at the elbows** and **frayed hems**; well-kept coats still **drape** like expensive weatherwear until the first hit.
