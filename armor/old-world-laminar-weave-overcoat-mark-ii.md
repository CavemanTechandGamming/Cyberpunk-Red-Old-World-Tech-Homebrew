# Old World Laminar-Weave Overcoat — Mark II

**Short description:** **Mid-game** refinements to laminar weave—tighter alignment mesh, better thermals, less drift than Mark I. Same silhouette, less babysitting.

**Armor:** Body

**Stopping power — Current:** 8  
**Stopping power — Max:** 8

**Armor penalty:** 0

## Long description

**Mark II** laminar coats use **stabilized pre-Collapse cell packs** in the weave and factory-correct **re-tension routines** that were rare in salvage days. Cold spots are fewer; overlock happens **less often** under the same punishment. Corporate security and well-funded crews treated Mark II as the **workhorse** layer before everything went to hell.

This is still **not** powered hardshell. No Agent. **Head** and **legs** are not covered unless you layer other gear.

**Appearance:** Same **long-coat** silhouette as Mark I, but the **rib pattern** in the weave reads **cleaner** under light—panels line up **tighter** at the seams. Common colors stay **charcoal, navy, and olive**, with occasional **slate or burgundy** lots. Collar and cuffs are **cut sharper**; hardware is still **low-profile**, sometimes with a **small manufacturer glyph** worn smooth on the inner tag. When the weave locks, the **snap** is a little **shorter** than Mark I, and the fabric returns to drape **faster**. Well-maintained Mark II coats **pass** as premium outerwear until someone looks at the **thread density** up close.

**Cost:** 1,250 eb (homebrew; mid-game tier—aligned with **Mark II** pricing in this homebrew line)
