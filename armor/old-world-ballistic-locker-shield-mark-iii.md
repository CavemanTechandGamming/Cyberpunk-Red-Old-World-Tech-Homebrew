## Long description

**Mark III** locker shields were bank-transfer and VIP-extraction kit: multi-hit ceramic stack, viscoelastic gel behind the strike face to eat impulse, and hinges rated for explosive overpressure near misses. The forearm lock distributes torque so your elbow survives angles that break lesser frames.

Still not networked—no corporate cloud handshake. What you get is materials science from the year everything still worked.

**Appearance:** Gunmetal face with almost no surface porosity—hits glaze white feathering instead of deep gouges until the gel saturates. The fold seam vanishes when closed; locking pins retract flush. The handle is overmolded alloy with drain channels; the strap uses mil tan or wolf grey that still holds dye. Open, the shield reads slightly wider than Mark I—more coverage without the weight of a wall.
