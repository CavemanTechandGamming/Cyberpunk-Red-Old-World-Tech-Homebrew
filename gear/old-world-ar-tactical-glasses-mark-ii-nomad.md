# Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark II (Nomad)

**Short description:** Mark II pre-Collapse AR glasses tuned for the road—full Agent replacement, improved optics vs Mark I, and HUD overlays for routes, weather, and convoy spacing.

## Description

A rare piece of Old World Tech, the **Mark II (Nomad)** augmented reality tactical glasses refine the Mark I road loadout. Worn on the face, they are intended as a **complete substitute for a pocket Agent**: comms, Data Pool access, apps, scheduling, recording, and ShortLink-style control live in the rig, so you do not need to carry a discrete Agent **while the glasses are powered and on your head**.

This variant biases the HUD toward **movement**: steadier heading bugs, clearer surface warnings, and gap readouts between vehicles with less jitter—convoy sense, not autopilot. Optics stay modest but improved: better distance judgment at speed than Mark I, **smoother zoom** on road hazards and signs—no implanted-grade suite.

They are **not** full cyberoptics. Unlike implanted cyberware, the system is entirely external—making it vulnerable to damage, interference, and theft. It will never match wired-in optics for speed, integration, or raw capability.

Power is easier to live with than on Mark I: **plan on recharging about every two to three days** under full HUD and Agent load, or **push toward a week** if you idle overlays between hauls—but let the battery go flat and the whole rig powers down, modifiers and all.

Highly valued by Edgerunners who want capability without chrome, and hunted by those who recognize their origin.

**Cost:** 1,250 eb (homebrew; mid-game tier)

## Custom Modifiers

While worn and operational, the glasses grant:

- **Movement:** +4
- **Drive Land Vehicle:** +2
- **Attack rolls:** +2
