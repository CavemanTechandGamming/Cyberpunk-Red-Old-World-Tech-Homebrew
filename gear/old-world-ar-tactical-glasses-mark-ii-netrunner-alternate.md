# Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark II (Netrunner Alternate)

**Alternate loadout:** Same chassis as **Mark II (Netrunner)**, but the Interface package emphasizes **pattern recognition and NET logic**—**Intelligence** as the primary modifier—instead of hardware-forward work (**Technology**).

**Short description:** Mark II pre-Collapse AR glasses tuned for NET work—full Agent replacement, improved optics vs Mark I, and HUD panels weighted toward ICE behavior, pathing, and abstract topology.

## Description

A rare piece of Old World Tech, the **Mark II (Netrunner Alternate)** augmented reality tactical glasses refine the Mark I Interface loadout. Worn on the face, they are intended as a **complete substitute for a pocket Agent**: comms, Data Pool access, apps, scheduling, recording, and ShortLink-style control live in the rig, so you do not need to carry a discrete Agent **while the glasses are powered and on your head**.

This variant biases the HUD toward **Interface cognition**: cleaner ICE-pattern hints, symbolic topology, and alert glyphs with less flicker—less “wrench turner,” more “chess in hot garbage.” Optics stay modest but improved: sharper glyph and text rendering than Mark I, **smoother zoom** on physical ports and labels when you still have to touch metal—no implanted-grade suite.

They are **not** full cyberoptics. Unlike implanted cyberware, the system is entirely external—making it vulnerable to damage, interference, and theft. It will never match wired-in optics for speed, integration, or raw capability.

Power is easier to live with than on Mark I: **plan on recharging about every two to three days** under full HUD and Agent load, or **push toward a week** if you idle overlays between runs—but let the battery go flat and the whole rig powers down, modifiers and all.

Highly valued by Edgerunners who want capability without chrome, and hunted by those who recognize their origin.
