# Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark I (Lawman)

**Short description:** Mark I pre-Collapse AR glasses tuned for enforcement—full Agent replacement, light optics, and HUD cues for warrants, IDs, and scene control.

## Description

A rare piece of Old World Tech, the **Mark I (Lawman)** augmented reality tactical glasses were built using advanced pre-Collapse technology. Worn on the face, they are intended as a **complete substitute for a pocket Agent**: comms, Data Pool access, apps, scheduling, recording, and ShortLink-style control live in the rig, so you do not need to carry a discrete Agent **while the glasses are powered and on your head**.

This variant biases the HUD toward **policing**: quick warrant and license prompts where databases still answer, rough crowd flow arrows, and priority tagging on weapons in view—street support, not department-issue neuralware. Optics stay modest: slightly better sightlines in flashing lights and rain, **usable zoom** on plates and faces at cordon distance, simple overlays—no implanted-grade suite.

They are **not** full cyberoptics. Unlike implanted cyberware, the system is entirely external—making it vulnerable to damage, interference, and theft. It will never match wired-in optics for speed, integration, or raw capability.

Power is another limitation: the Mark I runs on aging Old World cells. **Plan on recharging at least once per day** if you keep the HUD, full Agent loadout, and visual assists running the way they were meant to be used. If you strip back to minimal overlay and idle most Agent features between jobs, you can sometimes **stretch to about two days**—but let the battery go flat and the whole rig powers down, modifiers and all.

Highly valued by Edgerunners who want capability without chrome, and hunted by those who recognize their origin.
