# Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark II (Lawman)

**Short description:** Mark II pre-Collapse AR glasses tuned for enforcement—full Agent replacement, improved optics vs Mark I, and HUD cues for warrants, IDs, and scene control.

## Description

A rare piece of Old World Tech, the **Mark II (Lawman)** augmented reality tactical glasses refine the Mark I policing loadout. Worn on the face, they are intended as a **complete substitute for a pocket Agent**: comms, Data Pool access, apps, scheduling, recording, and ShortLink-style control live in the rig, so you do not need to carry a discrete Agent **while the glasses are powered and on your head**.

This variant biases the HUD toward **policing**: faster warrant and license prompts where databases still answer, cleaner crowd-flow cues, and priority tags on weapons in view with less stutter—street support, not department-issue neuralware. Optics stay modest but improved: better sightlines in flashing lights and rain than Mark I, **smoother zoom** on plates and faces at cordon distance—no implanted-grade suite.

They are **not** full cyberoptics. Unlike implanted cyberware, the system is entirely external—making it vulnerable to damage, interference, and theft. It will never match wired-in optics for speed, integration, or raw capability.

Power is easier to live with than on Mark I: **plan on recharging about every two to three days** under full HUD and Agent load, or **push toward a week** if you idle overlays between shifts—but let the battery go flat and the whole rig powers down, modifiers and all.

Highly valued by Edgerunners who want capability without chrome, and hunted by those who recognize their origin.

**Cost:** 1,250 eb (homebrew; mid-game tier)
