# Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark I (Generic)

**Short description:** Mark I pre-Collapse AR glasses that fully replace a separate Agent, while offering only a light stand-in for cyberoptics—clearer sight, modest zoom, and HUD readouts, not implanted-grade optics.

This is the **baseline Mark I** firmware and sensor pack. Role-specific variants tune the overlays and analytics without changing the overall power level. In this homebrew line, **marks** describe **power and fiction**, not fixed **eurodollar** prices—**Mark I** is **early game**, **Mark II** is **mid game** (see the matching Mark II files), and **Mark III** is **late game / legendary** (see Mark III files + `cyberwear/old-world-cranial-ar-dock-mark-iii.md`).

## Description

A rare piece of Old World Tech, the **Mark I** augmented reality tactical glasses were built using advanced pre-Collapse technology. Worn on the face, they are intended as a **complete substitute for a pocket Agent**: comms, Data Pool access, apps, scheduling, recording, and ShortLink-style control live in the rig, so you do not need to carry a discrete Agent **while the glasses are powered and on your head**.

They are **not** a full cyberoptic suite. What they give you is the **small stuff**—slightly sharper low-light and distance vision, **usable zoom** for reading plates or sighting, and **HUD overlays** for maps, tags, and tactical readouts. Handy, but nowhere near the breadth or punch of real cybereyes and stacked ocular cyberware; there is no neural pipe, only glass, sensors, and projection.

Unlike implanted cyberware, the system is entirely external—making it vulnerable to damage, interference, and theft. It will never match wired-in optics for speed, integration, or raw capability.

Power is another limitation: the Mark I runs on aging Old World cells. **Plan on recharging at least once per day** if you keep the HUD, full Agent loadout, and visual assists running the way they were meant to be used. If you strip back to minimal overlay and idle most Agent features between jobs, you can sometimes **stretch to about two days**—but let the battery go flat and the whole rig powers down, modifiers and all.

Highly valued by Edgerunners who want capability without chrome, and hunted by those who recognize their origin.
