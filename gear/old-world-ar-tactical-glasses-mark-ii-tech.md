# Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark II (Tech)

**Short description:** Mark II pre-Collapse AR glasses tuned for hands-on repair—full Agent replacement, improved optics vs Mark I, and HUD schematics for rigs, weapons, and chrome.

## Description

A rare piece of Old World Tech, the **Mark II (Tech)** augmented reality tactical glasses refine the Mark I shop loadout. Worn on the face, they are intended as a **complete substitute for a pocket Agent**: comms, Data Pool access, apps, scheduling, recording, and ShortLink-style control live in the rig, so you do not need to carry a discrete Agent **while the glasses are powered and on your head**.

This variant biases the HUD toward **fabrication and repair**: snappier exploded views, torque reminders, and stress coloring with less lag—shop assistant, not a fabber wired into your skull. Optics stay modest but improved: clearer fine detail up close than Mark I, **smoother zoom** on serials and micro-welds—no implanted-grade suite.

They are **not** full cyberoptics. Unlike implanted cyberware, the system is entirely external—making it vulnerable to damage, interference, and theft. It will never match wired-in optics for speed, integration, or raw capability.

Power is easier to live with than on Mark I: **plan on recharging about every two to three days** under full HUD and Agent load, or **push toward a week** if you idle overlays between jobs—but let the battery go flat and the whole rig powers down, modifiers and all.

Highly valued by Edgerunners who want capability without chrome, and hunted by those who recognize their origin.
