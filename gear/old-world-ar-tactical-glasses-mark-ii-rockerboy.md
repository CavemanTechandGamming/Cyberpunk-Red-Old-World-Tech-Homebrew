# Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark II (Rockerboy)

**Short description:** Mark II pre-Collapse AR glasses tuned for stage and crowd work—full Agent replacement, improved optics vs Mark I, and HUD cues for timing, acoustics, and presence.

## Description

A rare piece of Old World Tech, the **Mark II (Rockerboy)** augmented reality tactical glasses refine the Mark I performance loadout. Worn on the face, they are intended as a **complete substitute for a pocket Agent**: comms, Data Pool access, apps, scheduling, recording, and ShortLink-style control live in the rig, so you do not need to carry a discrete Agent **while the glasses are powered and on your head**.

This variant biases the HUD toward **performance**: tighter set lists, lyric prompts, crowd heat maps, stage lighting cues, and decibel readouts that update with less lag than Mark I—enough to sell a show, not enough to turn you into a studio. Optics stay modest but improved: steadier sightlines under harsh lights, **smoother zoom** to read faces at the edge of the pit, and cleaner AR overlays—no implanted-grade suite.

They are **not** full cyberoptics. Unlike implanted cyberware, the system is entirely external—making it vulnerable to damage, interference, and theft. It will never match wired-in optics for speed, integration, or raw capability.

Power is easier to live with than on Mark I: **plan on recharging about every two to three days** under full HUD and Agent load, or **push toward a week** if you idle overlays between gigs—but let the battery go flat and the whole rig powers down, modifiers and all.

Highly valued by Edgerunners who want capability without chrome, and hunted by those who recognize their origin.
