# Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark II (Media Alternate)

**Alternate loadout:** Same chassis as **Mark II (Media)**, but the package emphasizes **on-camera presence and spin**—**Cool** as the primary modifier—instead of deep-dive investigation (**Intelligence**).

**Short description:** Mark II pre-Collapse AR glasses tuned for stories—full Agent replacement, improved optics vs Mark I, and HUD tools for framing, audience heat, and live reads.

## Description

A rare piece of Old World Tech, the **Mark II (Media Alternate)** augmented reality tactical glasses refine the Mark I on-camera loadout. Worn on the face, they are intended as a **complete substitute for a pocket Agent**: comms, Data Pool access, apps, scheduling, recording, and ShortLink-style control live in the rig, so you do not need to carry a discrete Agent **while the glasses are powered and on your head**.

This variant biases the HUD toward **performance and optics**: smoother teleprompter rails, cleaner sentiment ticks on a crowd line, and cue cards for when to pivot—showrunner’s rig, not a library crawl. Optics stay modest but improved: steadier facial detail at interview distance than Mark I, **smoother zoom** on signage and stage cues—no implanted-grade suite.

They are **not** full cyberoptics. Unlike implanted cyberware, the system is entirely external—making it vulnerable to damage, interference, and theft. It will never match wired-in optics for speed, integration, or raw capability.

Power is easier to live with than on Mark I: **plan on recharging about every two to three days** under full HUD and Agent load, or **push toward a week** if you idle overlays between leads—but let the battery go flat and the whole rig powers down, modifiers and all.

Highly valued by Edgerunners who want capability without chrome, and hunted by those who recognize their origin.

**Cost:** 1,250 eb (homebrew; mid-game tier)
