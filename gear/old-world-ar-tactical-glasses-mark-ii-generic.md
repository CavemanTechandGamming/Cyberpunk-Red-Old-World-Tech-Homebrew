# Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark II (Generic)

**Short description:** Mark II pre-Collapse AR glasses—mid-tier Old World gear that fully replace a separate Agent and still only **lightly** substitute for cyberoptics, with better cells and a snappier HUD than Mark I.

This is the **baseline Mark II** firmware and sensor pack. Role-specific variants tune the overlays and analytics without changing the overall power level. In this homebrew line, **price steps up by mark:** Mark I is **early game**, **Mark II** is **mid game**, and **Mark III** is **late game / legendary** (see Mark III files + `cyberwear/old-world-cranial-ar-dock-mark-iii.md`).

## Description

A rare piece of Old World Tech, the **Mark II** augmented reality tactical glasses refine what the Mark I started. Worn on the face, they remain a **complete substitute for a pocket Agent**: comms, Data Pool access, apps, scheduling, recording, and ShortLink-style control live in the rig, so you do not need to carry a discrete Agent **while the glasses are powered and on your head**. Menus feel quicker, overlays judder less, and the Agent stack keeps up under stress better than first-generation salvage.

They are **not** a full cyberoptic suite. What they give you is still the **small stuff**—clearer low-light and distance vision than Mark I, **smoother zoom** for plates and sighting, and **HUD readouts** for maps, tags, and tactical cues. You are not getting cybereye parity; there is still no neural pipe, only glass, sensors, and projection.

Unlike implanted cyberware, the system is entirely external—making it vulnerable to damage, interference, and theft. It will never match wired-in optics for speed, integration, or raw capability.

Power is easier to live with than on Mark I: stabilized cell packs and better thermal management mean **plan on recharging about every two to three days** if you run the HUD, full Agent loadout, and visual assists hard. Strip back to minimal overlay and idle most Agent features between jobs and you can sometimes **push toward a week**—but let the battery go flat and the whole rig powers down, modifiers and all.

Highly valued by Edgerunners who want capability without chrome, and hunted by those who recognize their origin.

**Cost:** 1,250 eb (homebrew; mid-game tier)
