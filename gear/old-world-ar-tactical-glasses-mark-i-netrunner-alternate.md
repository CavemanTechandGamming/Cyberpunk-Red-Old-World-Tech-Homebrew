# Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark I (Netrunner Alternate)

**Alternate loadout:** Same chassis as **Mark I (Netrunner)**, but the Interface package emphasizes **pattern recognition and NET logic**—**Intelligence** as the primary modifier—instead of hardware-forward work (**Technology**).

**Short description:** Mark I pre-Collapse AR glasses tuned for NET work—full Agent replacement, light optics, and HUD panels weighted toward ICE behavior, pathing, and abstract topology.

## Description

A rare piece of Old World Tech, the **Mark I (Netrunner Alternate)** augmented reality tactical glasses were built using advanced pre-Collapse technology. Worn on the face, they are intended as a **complete substitute for a pocket Agent**: comms, Data Pool access, apps, scheduling, recording, and ShortLink-style control live in the rig, so you do not need to carry a discrete Agent **while the glasses are powered and on your head**.

This variant biases the HUD toward **Interface cognition**: priority hints for ICE patterns, symbolic topology, and alert glyphs tuned to how the NET *behaves*—less “wrench turner,” more “chess in hot garbage.” Optics stay modest: slightly sharper glyph and text rendering, **usable zoom** on physical ports and labels when you still have to touch metal, simple AR overlays—no implanted-grade suite.

They are **not** full cyberoptics. Unlike implanted cyberware, the system is entirely external—making it vulnerable to damage, interference, and theft. It will never match wired-in optics for speed, integration, or raw capability.

Power is another limitation: the Mark I runs on aging Old World cells. **Plan on recharging at least once per day** if you keep the HUD, full Agent loadout, and visual assists running the way they were meant to be used. If you strip back to minimal overlay and idle most Agent features between jobs, you can sometimes **stretch to about two days**—but let the battery go flat and the whole rig powers down, modifiers and all.

Highly valued by Edgerunners who want capability without chrome, and hunted by those who recognize their origin.

**Cost:** **GM / table.** **Mark I** is **early-game** in this line; set **eurodollars** to your economy (not fixed here).
**Suggested price:** 550 eb — *baseline for balance; change freely to fit your campaign.*
