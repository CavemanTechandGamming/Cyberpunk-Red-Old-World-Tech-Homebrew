# Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark II (Exec)

**Short description:** Mark II pre-Collapse AR glasses tuned for leverage—full Agent replacement, improved optics vs Mark I, and HUD readouts for people, org charts, and policy traps.

## Description

A rare piece of Old World Tech, the **Mark II (Exec)** augmented reality tactical glasses refine the Mark I corporate loadout. Worn on the face, they are intended as a **complete substitute for a pocket Agent**: comms, Data Pool access, apps, scheduling, recording, and ShortLink-style control live in the rig, so you do not need to carry a discrete Agent **while the glasses are powered and on your head**.

This variant biases the HUD toward **corporate maneuvering**: snappier meeting timelines, stakeholder tags, and boilerplate reminders from open filings—briefing material, not a seat on the board. Optics stay modest but improved: steadier reads on micro-expressions across a table than Mark I, **smoother zoom** on badges and fine print—no implanted-grade suite.

They are **not** full cyberoptics. Unlike implanted cyberware, the system is entirely external—making it vulnerable to damage, interference, and theft. It will never match wired-in optics for speed, integration, or raw capability.

Power is easier to live with than on Mark I: **plan on recharging about every two to three days** under full HUD and Agent load, or **push toward a week** if you idle overlays between meetings—but let the battery go flat and the whole rig powers down, modifiers and all.

Highly valued by Edgerunners who want capability without chrome, and hunted by those who recognize their origin.
