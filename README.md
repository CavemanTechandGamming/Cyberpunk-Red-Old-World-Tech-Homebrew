# Old World Tech Homebrew

Supplementary **Cyberpunk RED** content: gear, cyberware, and other items themed as **Old World technology**—hardware and software from **before the DataKrash and the Collapse**, later **salvaged**, **repurposed**, and in some cases **updated** by post-Collapse techs, corps, and edgerunners. This repository is a structured collection for campaigns that want salvage-tier gear with a clear power curve and consistent fiction.

---

## The premise

**Old World tech** is not the same as off-the-shelf Night City junk. It was built for a world that still had intact infrastructure, supply chains, and R&D budgets. Most of it **did not survive** the Collapse intact. What remains is often:

- **Buried** in ruins, military caches, corporate vaults, or old transit tunnels  
- **Incomplete**—missing manuals, firmware keys, or legal software licenses  
- **Dangerous** to run—power regulation, thermal limits, and security models that assume a world that no longer exists  

**Repurposing** is the norm: a ripperdoc or a hot-fix crew figures out how to **make it work anyway**—sometimes by **grafting** modern components (Agents, ShortLink, aftermarket cells) onto Old World frames. **Updates** show up as **marks** and **variants**: same lineage, better integration, or a different philosophy (cheap charge tether versus full cranial dock).

This collection treats that progression as **explicit**: early-game salvage, mid-game stabilized kits, late-game legendary refits that only make sense with **supporting chrome** or **specialized infrastructure**.

---

## Marks and power curve (this homebrew line)

Across the items statted here, **marks** are a shorthand for **how far** the community—or a single obsessed tech—has pushed a design:

| Tier | Role in the story | Typical feel |
|------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Mark I** | Early salvage / first stable loadouts | Affordable, hungry for power, still “external” and stealable |
| **Mark II** | Mid-game refinement | Better cells, thermals, HUD latency; still worn like normal glasses |
| **Mark III** | Late-game / legendary | Often **refit** to depend on **serious support** (for example a **cranial dock**)—no more “pocket gadget” fantasy |

**Companion tech** (charge tethers, docks) exists so the fiction stays grounded: you can **ease bookkeeping** or **unlock** the top tier without pretending a pair of lenses runs forever on vibes alone.

## Repository layout

Content is grouped by **category** (all Markdown), **A–Z** by folder name:

| Folder | Contents |
|--------|----------|
| `ammo/` | Ammunition (reserved for future entries). |
| `armor/` | Armor and related protection (including the **laminar-weave overcoat**, Marks I–III). |
| `clothing/` | Clothing (reserved for future entries). |
| `cyberwear/` | Cyberware—implants that interface with Old World gear (charge tethers, cranial dock, etc.). |

| `vehicles/` | Vehicles (reserved for future entries). |
| `weapons/` | Weapons (reserved for future entries). |

## Spotlight: Old World AR tactical glasses

The flagship line in this repository is a family of **AR glasses** that can **replace a pocket Agent** and offer **modest** stand-ins for cyberoptics—never full cybereye parity, by design. Role-tuned files tweak HUD bias (Solo vs Netrunner vs Media, and so on). **Alternate** files swap the **primary stat** emphasis where a build might prefer a different attribute.

**Cyberware companions:**

- **Charge tether (Mark I / Mark II)** — Power-only; Mark II adds **charge conditioning** and **thermal / overcharge** protection versus Mark I.  
- **Cranial dock (Mark III)** — Full **dock** for refit **Mark III** frames; **refit Mark III glasses** in this homebrew are **inert** without it.

Exact stats, costs, and Humanity Costs are **homebrew**; adjust to your table’s economy and tone.
