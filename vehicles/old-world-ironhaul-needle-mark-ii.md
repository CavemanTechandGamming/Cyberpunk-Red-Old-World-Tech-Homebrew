# Old World Ironhaul Needle — Mark II

**Short description:** **Refurbished Ironhaul Needle**—still **Bad Larry Long**: **coffin sleeper**, **double-stretched** frame, **dual saddle tanks**, **Class 8** **needle-nose** **tractor** with air system honest, cooling stack sorted, and cab metal that stops flapping at speed. Still **heavy-truck** **Speed**; **no** **trailer** on the base sheet unless your table adds one.

## Long description

**Mark II** is what happens when someone with shop access and patience treats the **Needle** like infrastructure instead of a folklore prop—without cutting the stretch or throwing away the coffin to pretend the truck fits a city. Brake cans, lines, radiator stack, turbo seals, and the gremlin in the aftertreatment story get addressed before the polish. The **inline-six** still rattles windows at idle—love it or lease something boring.

Fiction-wise, this is the rig a nomad family names when the convoy needs torque, the bunk needs a door that locks, and the AV lanes are not an option. **Bad Larry Long** still answers on the band.

**Appearance:** Cab gaps even; stacks straight; chrome either restored or honestly replaced with steel that will not lie. The **coffin sleeper** gets seals that actually seal; **dual tanks** cleaned, tested, and repainted so the left-right match reads deliberate again. Paint might be fleet livery—deep blue, wine red, or corporate white—without pretending it is new. Fifth wheel serviced; gladhands ready to gossip. Inside: seats that hold, gauges that agree, coffin bunk foam replaced where rats won, smell of clean oil instead of a lawsuit.

**Cost:** **GM / table.** **Mark II** should track **mid-tier** heavy commercial iron in *your* economy—more than a beater flatbed, less than a corp armored convoy unless you say otherwise.

**Suggested price:** 88,000 eb — *baseline for balance; change freely to fit your campaign.*
