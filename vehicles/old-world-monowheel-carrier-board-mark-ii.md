# Old World Monowheel Carrier Board — Mark II

**Short description:** **Refurbished** pre-Collapse monowheel deck: gyro logic stabilized, thermal management back on speaking terms with the motor, and a load spine that can take punishment without twisting the ring. Still one rider—still zero cabin—but it feels like a **tool** again instead of a gamble.

## Long description

The **Mark II** line is what you get when a hot-fix crew **replaces cells**, **reseats bearings**, and **reloads factory curves** from a half-corrupted archive. The wheel still hums, but the hum is **predictable**. Lean inputs stop fighting you; the deck damps small shocks so your cargo does not walk off the straps mid-corner.

It is the same Old World chassis fantasy—**one wheel, open deck, courier blood**—with enough reliability that a fixer will put a client on it without writing a condolence letter first. It will not survive a tank shell; it will survive **Night City’s ordinary cruelty** long enough to matter.

**Appearance:** The ring wears **cleaner fairing segments**—often mismatched—where someone replaced impact crushes. The deck spine is **re-machined** or laminated with newer alloy strips, edges deburred, tie-down slots **not** chewed to razors. The HUD puck may be swapped for a **brighter aftermarket face** with lean heat-map and cell health bars. It still looks like **salvage**, but salvage someone **loved** for a weekend.

**Cost:** **GM / table.** **Mark II** sits where **mid-tier personal transport** belongs in *your* economy—compare to bikes, beater cars, and other vehicles at the table, not to armor or glasses marks by default.
**Suggested price:** 1,000 eb — *baseline for balance; change freely to fit your campaign.*
