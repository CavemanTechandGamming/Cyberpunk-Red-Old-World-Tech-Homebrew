# Old World Bessie — Mark II

**Short description:** **Refurbished Bessie**—same **extended-cab** **long-bed** **RWD** **pickup**, still named **Bessie**, still on the **muscle** **Speed** column. **Mark II** means the **318-class** build runs honest: cooling, charging, and ECU maps back on speaking terms with the block—and the **two-tone blue** finally reads intentional again.

## Long description

**Mark II** is what happens when someone with shop access treats **Bessie** like a partner instead of a dare. Bushings, brake lines, radiator, alternator, and the racing computer’s ghost codes get addressed before the wax. The **small-block** still lopes—**stage-two** cam flavor does not vanish—but it fires when asked and stops pulling timing out of spite.

Fiction-wise, this is the truck a nomad cousin brags about without opening the hood: long bed for gear, cab for crew, noise for anyone who needs a reminder they are alive.

**Appearance:** Two-tone blue resprayed or cut-and-buffed so the seam reads clean again. Bed rails straightened or replaced; dent map reduced to “character.” Wheels match; tires round. Interior vacuumed into politeness; seat foam honest. Still **Bessie**—just less apology.

**Cost:** **GM / table.** **Mark II** should track **mid-tier** muscle and performance trucks in *your* economy—more than a beater farm hauler, less than a fresh import supercar unless you say otherwise.

**Suggested price:** 22,000 eb — *baseline for balance; change freely to fit your campaign.*

**Structural damage points:** 72

**Seats:** 5

**Rooms:** 1

**Speed:** 77

**Drivetrain:** Rear-wheel drive

**Powerplant:** Built small-block V8 (~318 cu. in. class—factory line spec for this homebrew); factory stage-two cam (flavor); aftermarket racing computer
