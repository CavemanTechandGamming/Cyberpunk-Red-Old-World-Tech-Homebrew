# Old World Bessie — Mark III

**Short description:** **Legendary-refit** **Bessie**—**extended cab**, **long bed**, **RWD**, **muscle** **Speed** column. **Mark III** is the **two-tone blue** hauler that survived the timeline: regulated power, hardened where it matters, still a pickup first and a brag second.

## Long description

**Mark III** is not “a nicer truck.” It is what you build when you keep the **318-class** soul and bolt on everything that makes the Collapse shut up: fuel system that forgives garbage, brakes that forgive panic, subframe honesty under a skin that still reads pre-Collapse Americana.

The silhouette stays **Bessie**: long bed, extra cab glass, hood long enough to hide bad decisions. Five seats for crew or cargo that needs seat belts; the bed still swallows sheets, crates, or a body wrapped in tarps—your campaign’s ethics, not mine.

At the table, treat **Mark III** as a serious ground asset: loud in reputation, expensive in maintenance, and a magnet for anyone who recognizes Old World cam part numbers through soot.

**Appearance:** Two-tone blue deep enough to look like midnight over harbour—ceramic feel on the clearcoat, seam laser-straight. Glass laminated where it matters; rubber flush. Wheels period-correct in silhouette, modern in strength. Bed liner integrated; tie-downs not chewed to razors. Inside: seats that hold you, redundant power taps, a racing computer face that looks civilian until you scroll maps. Wrong in the details if you know where to look.

**Cost:** **GM / table.** **Mark III** can be a major capital expense—on par with serious cyberware packages or a fixer’s favorite low-profile hauler if your economy supports it. Calibrate openly.

**Suggested price:** 48,000 eb — *baseline for balance; change freely to fit your campaign.*

**Structural damage points:** 88

**Seats:** 5

**Rooms:** 1

**Speed:** 90

**Drivetrain:** Rear-wheel drive

**Powerplant:** Built small-block V8 (~318 cu. in. class—factory line spec for this homebrew); factory stage-two cam (flavor); aftermarket racing computer
