# Old World Redline Viceroy — Mark I

**Short description:** Pre-Collapse **rear-wheel-drive** **2+2** muscle coupe from the upscale end of the line—wedge glass, optional **T-tops**, and a **350-class** **small-block V8** that still thinks lead is a food group. **Mark I** means patina, oil spots, and grins.

## Long description

Before the Collapse, the **Redline Viceroy** sat where pony cars tried to be civilized: not the raw track fantasy, but the trim with softer springs, plusher seats, and enough brightwork to pretend you were grown up—while the short block under the hood still refused to apologize.

**Factory spec** for this homebrew line is **rear-wheel drive only** and a **beefy small-block V8** in the **~350 cubic-inch** class—carb or early EFI flavor, your table’s call. Use **mid-1980s American pony-coupe** proportions as a **visual** anchor—**calendar year** is flavor for your group, not a stat in **2045**. **RS-style** appearance (body-color trim, aero add-ons, rear spoiler) reads as factory attitude without pretending the car is subtle.

**Mark I** is salvage truth: valve tick you name, a third gear that negotiates, fog lamps that fog, and T-top seals that weep when it rains. It is still a date car if your date likes noise.

**Appearance:** Low wedge, long hood, short deck—chrome trim pitted, urethane nose and tail fascias scuffed where parking-lot curbs won. T-tops off in hand, or a fixed roof if your lot never ordered glass. Wheels are steel or period alloys, curbed honest. Interior velour or half-leather split by plastic that squeaks; optional digital dash segments dead or lying. Oil perfume and hot dust.

**Cost:** **GM / table.** **Mark I** muscle-coupe salvage—cheap entry if you ignore what breaks next.

**Suggested price:** 9,500 eb — *baseline for balance; change freely to fit your campaign.*

**Rooms:** 1

**Speed:** 50

**Drivetrain:** Rear-wheel drive

**Powerplant:** Small-block V8 (~350 cu. in. class—factory line spec for this homebrew)
