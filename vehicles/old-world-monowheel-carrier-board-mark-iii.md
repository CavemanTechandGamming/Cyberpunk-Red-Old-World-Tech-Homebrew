# Old World Monowheel Carrier Board — Mark III

**Short description:** A **legendary-refit** monowheel carrier: **mag-assist clutch** in the hub, battlefield-grade gyro stack, and a deck spine rated for loads that should have required a van. One rider, no cabin—**pure Old World overkill** pointed at the last mile.

## Long description

**Mark III** is the version you chase when a rumor says **pre-Collapse military surplus** bled into the street. The ring is not just spinning—it is **managed**: magnetic trim keeps the tire planted when physics says “dump it,” and the controller **predicts** lean before your inner ear finishes complaining.

It is still **not** a car. There are **no rooms**—only a deck, a wheel, and the rider’s nerve. What changes is the **margin**: the kind of cargo you dare to carry, the chase you dare to stay inside, the gap you dare to thread between two AV washouts.

Power is hungry and attitude is worse. Treat it like a **flagship** in your garage: people will recognize the ring note, the fairing seam, the way it holds a line.

**Appearance:** The ring shows **layered fairing** with service latches—milspec olive, storm gray, or illegally stripped corporate white. The deck spine is **wide, chamfered**, sometimes **rubberized** for wet boots. The hub may carry a **visible mag coil halo** (pure fiction flavor) and subtle **warning glyphs** in Old World script. It looks **expensive**, **dangerous**, and **maintained by someone who still has access to tools**.

**Cost:** **GM / table.** **Mark III** refits can be **major** purchases—often **well above** what you charge for a legendary jacket or glasses if vehicles are rare in your campaign. Calibrate with the table openly.
**Suggested price:** 3,500 eb — *baseline for balance; change freely to fit your campaign.*

**Structural damage points:** 42

**Seats:** 1

**Rooms:** 1

**Speed:** 44
