# Old World Redline Viceroy — Mark III

**Short description:** **Legendary-refit** **Redline Viceroy**—**RWD**, **350-class** **small-block** **2+2** myth with hardened subsystems, regulated power, and the quiet confidence of a pony coupe that was never supposed to survive this era. Still **rear-wheel drive only**; the fiction is torque and exit lines, not all-wheel traction.

## Long description

**Mark III** is not “a nicer showroom fantasy.” It is what you build when you cherry-pick the best Old World drivetrain parts still on pallets—limited-slip or locker behavior that still makes sense on wet asphalt, cooling and oiling that forgive traffic and gunfire-adjacent sprints, chassis bracing and discreet armor under skin that still reads like a night-out car, and a **small-block** tune that pulls like it remembers when gas was cheap.

The silhouette stays honest: long hood, short deck, optional **T-top** channels or a fixed roof—your salvage story’s call. Four seats for crew, dates, or witnesses; nobody pretends the rear is a limo.

At the table, treat **Mark III** as a serious ground asset: expensive for a coupe, loud in reputation even when the exhaust is baffled polite, and a magnet for anyone who recognizes pre-Collapse casting numbers.

**Appearance:** Paint is deep—gunmetal pearl, midnight blue, or flat black with a ceramic feel. Glass is laminated thick; rubber sits flush. Wheels are period-correct in silhouette but modern in strength; tires are depth-legal and sticky when you pay for them. Bumpers and urethane fascias integrate with the armored story without erasing the 1980s wedge. Inside: seats that hold you without squeaking theater, a dash that lights consistently, redundant power taps for gear. Wrong in the details if you know where to look.

**Cost:** **GM / table.** **Mark III** can be a major capital expense—on par with serious cyberware packages or a fixer’s favorite ride if your economy supports it. Calibrate openly.

**Suggested price:** 42,000 eb — *baseline for balance; change freely to fit your campaign.*

**Structural damage points:** 62

**Seats:** 4

**Rooms:** 1

**Speed:** 90

**Drivetrain:** Rear-wheel drive

**Powerplant:** Small-block V8 (~350 cu. in. class—factory line spec for this homebrew)
