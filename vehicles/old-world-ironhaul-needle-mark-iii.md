# Old World Ironhaul Needle — Mark III

**Short description:** **Legendary-refit** **Ironhaul Needle**—**Bad Larry Long** with mil-surplus discipline where it matters, regulated power, and the quiet confidence of a rig that was never supposed to outlive the map. **Coffin sleeper**, **double-stretched** frame, and **dual saddle tanks** stay in the silhouette—this line is not pretending to be a day cab. Still **heavy-truck** **Speed**; **tractor-only** in the base sheet unless your table bolts on a trailer.

## Long description

**Mark III** is not “a nicer semi.” It is what you build when you steal the best Old World drivetrain parts still on pallets—**tandem** hardware that still locks under load, **turbodiesel** management that loves garbage fuel, armored inserts under skin that still reads like a working truck, climate control that filters smoke like it is personal, and suspension that forgives potholes that used to bend rims—without amputating the **stretch** or the **coffin** because **Bad Larry Long** is the whole point.

The silhouette stays honest: needle nose, long hood, stacks that mean business, coffin sleeper standing tall behind the cab, dual tanks flanking a frame that took two stretches to become legend, fifth wheel ready for whatever your campaign hooks up—dry van, flatbed, tanker, or a lie wrapped in tarps.

At the table, treat **Mark III** as a flagship ground asset: expensive, loud in reputation even when the diesel note is tuned polite, and a magnet for anyone who recognizes pre-Collapse casting numbers on a water pump.

**Appearance:** Paint is deep—gunmetal pearl, storm grey, or flat agency black with a ceramic feel. Glass is laminated thick; cab corners sit flush. **Coffin sleeper** integrates with the armored story without erasing the 1960s needle-nose your brain remembers. **Dual tanks** match; **frame rails** show honest care instead of rust theater. Inside: seats that do not stink, redundant power taps for gear, a bunk that still smells like hope. Wrong in the details if you know where to look.

**Cost:** **GM / table.** **Mark III** can be a major capital expense—on par with serious cyberware packages or a convoy lead vehicle if your economy supports it. Calibrate openly.
