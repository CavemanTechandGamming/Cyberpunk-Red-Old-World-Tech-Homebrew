# Old World Meridian Lanehart — Mark I

**Short description:** Pre-Collapse **front-wheel-drive** four-door **midsize sedan**—**V6** up front, **automatic** or **manual** flavor, and the kind of **boring reliability** that outlasted three owners and two affairs. **Mark I** means taxi miles, clearcoat failure, and a **check engine** light that speaks in tongues.

## Long description

Before the Collapse, the **Meridian Lanehart** was the answer to a deeply Old World question: *can I commute, merge onto a freeway, and pick up groceries without pretending I am in a music video?* It was not exotic—double-wishbone fuss up front on better lots, disc brakes where it mattered, and a **dual-cam V6** in the **~3.0 liter** class that pulled smoothly instead of shouting.

**Factory spec** for this homebrew line is **front-wheel drive** and that **V6** layout—**calendar year** is flavor for your table (think **late 1990s–early 2000s** global midsize four-door proportions), not a stat in **2045**. No **AWD** in the baseline—this is the plain family-adjacent trim, not the rare-weather variant.

**Mark I** is salvage truth: transmission flare on the 2–3 shift, motor mounts oil-soaked, suspension clunk over transit tracks, and cabin air that tastes like spilled coffee and regret. It still starts most mornings—which is more than you can say for your ex.

**Appearance:** Soft wedge nose, tall greenhouse, trunk lid long enough to sleep on in a pinch. Paint is silver, champagne, or that green that looked classy in the lot and sad in the sun. Plastic trim fades to chalk; alloy wheels if ordered, steel with covers if life won. Interior beige or gray cloth or leather cracked at the bolsters; woodgrain appliqué peeling at the edges. Aftermarket head unit held in by hope.
