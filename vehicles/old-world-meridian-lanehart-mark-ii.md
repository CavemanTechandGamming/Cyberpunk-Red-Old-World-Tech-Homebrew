# Old World Meridian Lanehart — Mark II

**Short description:** **Refurbished Meridian Lanehart**—**front-wheel-drive** **V6** four-door with the invisible car’s dignity back: shifts clean, cooling honest, interior sanitized so it does not smell like a fryer vent. Still **FWD** in the factory line—no **AWD** retrofit unless your table says so.

## Long description

**Mark II** is what happens when someone with shop access treats the **Lanehart** like infrastructure instead of a disposable shell. Bushings, brake lines, radiator, axle boots, and the idle-air gremlin get addressed before the wax. The **V6** still whispers through factory or period-correct exhaust—smooth torque, not a muscle car bark.

Fiction-wise, this is the sedan a fixer’s cousin drives when the job needs four doors, a trunk, and “I am absolutely not a threat” energy—until the V6 wakes up on an on-ramp.

**Appearance:** Panel gaps even; trim replaced where sun killed it. Glass clear enough to read plates. Wheels match; tires round instead of faith-based. Interior carpet shampooed; headliner no longer sags like a tired mouth. Someone swapped the head unit for a ShortLink-friendly face—or left it stock to blend. From the curb: respectable commuter metal, not a scream.

**Cost:** **GM / table.** **Mark II** should track **mid-tier** sedans and practical coupes in *your* economy—more than a beater hatch, less than a fresh executive import unless you say otherwise.

**Suggested price:** 16,000 eb — *baseline for balance; change freely to fit your campaign.*

**Structural damage points:** 50

**Seats:** 5

**Rooms:** 1

**Speed:** 75

**Drivetrain:** Front-wheel drive

**Powerplant:** V6 (~3.0 L class—factory line spec for this homebrew)
