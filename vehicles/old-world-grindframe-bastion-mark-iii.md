# Old World Grindframe Bastion — Mark III

**Short description:** **Legendary-refit** **Grindframe Bastion**—**4WD**, **turbodiesel**, **full-frame** three-row myth with **mil-surplus hardening**, **regulated power**, and the quiet confidence of a vehicle that was never supposed to survive this era. **Factory heavy bumpers** stay in the silhouette—now married to armor logic, not mall chrome. Still **eight seats**; now the cabin feels like **policy** instead of apology.

## Long description

**Mark III** is not “a nicer SUV.” It is what you build when you steal the best Old World subsystems still on pallets—**four-wheel-drive** hardware that still locks when you need it, **turbodiesel** management that loves garbage fuel, armored inserts under skin that still reads civilian, climate control that filters smoke like it is personal, and suspension that forgives curbs that used to bend rims.

The silhouette stays honest: long roof, upright glass, steel bumper faces that still look like they mean business, rear opening you could load a casket through—metaphorically or literally, your campaign’s call. The third row is for operators, witnesses, or inventory; nobody pretends it is a penalty box anymore.

At the table, treat **Mark III** as a flagship ground asset: expensive, loud in reputation even when the diesel note is tuned polite, and a magnet for anyone who recognizes pre-Collapse part numbers.

**Appearance:** Paint is deep—gunmetal pearl, storm grey, or flat agency black with a ceramic feel. Glass is laminated thick; frames sit flush. Front and rear bumpers are integrated with the armored story—hoops, steps, and recovery points preserved so it still reads like the early-2000s off-road trim your brain remembers, not a flat sheet-metal apology. Running boards are integrated, not a bolted afterthought. The roof might carry low-profile racks and antenna farms that fold flat. Inside: leather or synthetic that does not stink, console touches that hint at armor, redundant power taps for gear. Wrong in the details if you know where to look.

**Cost:** **GM / table.** **Mark III** can be a major capital expense—on par with serious cyberware packages or small property plays if your economy supports it. Calibrate openly.
