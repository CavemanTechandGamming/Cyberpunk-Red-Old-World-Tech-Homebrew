# Old World Pitlane Spanjack — Mark I

**Short description:** Pre-Collapse **low-profile** **hydraulic floor jack**—high reach for the lift, slim saddle for garage clearance, steel frame and wheels so one tech can roll it anywhere. Statted as a **vehicle** entry for sheet parity; **Speed** is **10**, same **Companion** tier as a **bicycle** (rolled by hand, not driven).

## Long description

Before the Collapse, **Pitlane Spanjack** units sat in every serious bay: long-stroke ram, pump handle that still had knurl, release valve you could find without bleeding, and casters that did not squeak like a rat with a megaphone. Low profile slides under aired-out sports coupe bellies; high reach still lifts a loaded subframe when the book says do not try this at home.

Think long-reach, low-saddle pit jack flavor—**Mark I** is honest salvage: seals weep, hydraulic fluid smells like a chemical childhood, wheels flat-spotted, and paint gone to patina that lies about how gentle the last owner was.

**Appearance:** Steel U-frame in red, blue, or industrial grey gone to rust freckles. Pump handle wood or plastic cracked at the grip. Saddle pad split or missing; someone bolted a hard rubber block that is not OSHA but is honest. Small stubborn wheels—roll speed on the sheet tracks bicycle-tier movement when you shove it across oil-stained concrete.

**Cost:** **GM / table.** **Mark I** shop jack salvage—cheap next to a bicycle, expensive if you need it before the ceiling comes down.
