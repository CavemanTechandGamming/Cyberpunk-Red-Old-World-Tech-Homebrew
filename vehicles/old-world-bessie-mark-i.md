# Old World Bessie — Mark I

**Short description:** Pre-Collapse **rear-wheel-drive** **full-size pickup**—**extended cab**, **long bed**—named **Bessie** in every story worth telling. **Mark I** is a **built** **small-block V8** in the **~318 cu. in.** class with a **factory stage-two cam** and an **aftermarket racing computer** that lies about being tame. Despite the **pickup** body, this line uses the **muscle** **Speed** column. **Two-tone blue**, **weathered**—pretty only if you squint.

## Long description

Before the Collapse, somebody ordered a **work truck** and then caught religion at the wrong speed shop. **Bessie** is not subtle: **long bed** for lumber, **extended cab** for witnesses, and a **V8** that sounds like it is picking a fight with the whole block.

**Factory sheet** for this homebrew is **rear-wheel drive**—use **late-1980s–early-1990s American** full-size half-ton proportions as **visual** flavor; **calendar year** is not a stat in **2045**. **Mark I** here is the hot-rodded salvage: **cam** grind in the **factory stage-two** performance flavor, paired with a **racing ECU** or piggyback that does not apologize for fuel maps. At the table that reads as a **built** **318-class** small-block, lumpy idle, and second-gear grins.

**Mark I** is honest wear: bed rails scarred, cab corners dented where a loader said no, oil seep that marks its territory, and brakes that remember every panic stop since the turn of the century.

**Appearance:** Two-tone blue—navy over robin’s-egg or faded periwinkle over steel, your salvage story’s call—with the break at the belt where clearcoat gave up. Sheet metal dull, trim pitted, bumpers chrome or argent plastic gone grey. Steel wheels or period slots, tires with sidewalls that tell lies. Interior bench or split front, rear jump seats with springs honest about age. Weathered like a tool that outlasted three owners and one divorce.
