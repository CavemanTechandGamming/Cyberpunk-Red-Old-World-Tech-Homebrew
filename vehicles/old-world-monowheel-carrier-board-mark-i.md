# Old World Monowheel Carrier Board — Mark I

**Short description:** A pre-Collapse **single-wheel cargo deck** with a fat-ring motor and basic gyro assist—built for **last-mile courier hauls**, not highway glory. Salvage runners strap crates to it, stand or lean through traffic, and learn which potholes will throw the ring.

## Long description

Before the Collapse, fleets of these **monowheel carrier boards** threaded arcologies and service tunnels: one rider, one big wheel, a flat load spine with hardpoints. The **Mark I** state in this homebrew line is **honest salvage**—the firmware still talks to the gyro, but the cells sag, the bearings sing, and the safety interlocks were **ripped out** somewhere in the last thirty years.

You do not “drive” it like a car; you **balance** it. Lean forward to load the ring, feather the deck tilt, and let the cargo straps take the slap. It is fast enough to matter in a chase, slow enough to get caught if you are cocky. It shines on **broken streets and narrow cuts** where a four-wheeler cannot follow.

**Appearance:** A **broad ring** dominates the silhouette—scuffed composite or oxidized alloy—with a **narrow standing deck** bolted across the hub line. The rider’s pad is worn leather or cracked foam over a **spine rail** punched for straps. Control is **bars or side stalks** (your table’s flavor) wired into a **weather-cracked HUD puck** that still shows speed, lean, and battery paranoia. Paint is faded corporate livery or none; every unit looks like it survived a war because it did.

**Cost:** 550 eb (homebrew; early-game tier)

**Structural damage points:** 26

**Seats:** 1

**Rooms:** 0

**Speed:** 5
