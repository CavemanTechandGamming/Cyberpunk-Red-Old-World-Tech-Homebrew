# Old World Cranial AR Dock — Mark III Interface

**Short description:** A cranial implant that **docks** compatible **Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark III**, feeding them **all** power and high-bandwidth data. **Required** for factory-refit Mark III glasses to function at all.

**Type:** Cyberware (implant)  
**Install:** Clinic or better (mastoid / temporal hardware; not street chop)  
**Cost:** **GM / table.** Set **eurodollars** to your economy relative to **Mark III** glasses and other cyberware at your table.  
**Suggested price:** 750 eb — *baseline for balance; change freely to fit your campaign.*
**Humanity Cost:** 4 (or 1d6/2 if installed after character creation—GM choice)

## Description

Pre-Collapse militaries and airframers prototyped **face computers** that could **mate** with the skull instead of living off fragile earpiece batteries. This implant is the receiving half: paired **docking posts** and a short **high-rate bus** tied into your existing neural interface stack (typically routed through **cyberaudio** pathways—assumed compatible at your table).

**Legendary Mark III glasses in this homebrew line are refit dock-only:** their internal power is **gone**, and the frame **refuses** to boot off-body. When those glasses lock in, they do not just “sit” on your nose—they **seat**: mechanical alignment, magnetic snap, and optical-data coupling. **Everything** runs off this implant’s regulated tap (fed by your body’s cyberware power budget the way other chrome does). There is **no** separate battery workflow for that refit.

Bandwidth is where the money is: ShortLink and Data Pool through the seated rig feel like they finally belong in the same decade as your reflexes. It still is not a cybereye replacement, but the gap narrows **while docked**.

**Compatibility:** Built for **refit Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark III** (see `gear/old-world-ar-tactical-glasses-mark-iii-*.md`). Older marks (Mark I / II) use different frames and connectors; they **do not** gain these benefits and may not seat safely—do not force it.

## Docked vs. undocked

**Mark III docked (refit glasses locked into this implant)**  
- **Power & data:** The glasses **function**; modifiers are **only** on the glasses entries (below).  
- **No routine battery tracking** for the seated rig unless the GM imposes deprivation (jail, Faraday cage, sabotage, blown implant).

**Refit Mark III glasses without this dock / wrong mark / no glasses**  
- **Refit Mark III frames:** **Inert**—no HUD, no Agent, **no** modifiers from the glasses files.  
- The implant alone is mostly **dead weight** socially (tiny ports, faint scar lines). It does not power random eyewear.  
- Optional fluff: occasional **ache** or **RF whistle** near strong jammers (GM flavor).

## Risks and limitations

- **Surgery footprint:** Another entry point for **trauma, EM interference, and malware** that targets headware.  
- **Theft still exists:** Someone can rip your **Mark III** off your face mid-dock if they win the grab—then you are missing both the tool and the synergy until you recover or replace.  
- **Not full cyberoptics:** You are still looking through lenses, not neural pipes.

## Custom Modifiers

**None on the implant alone.** All numerical bonuses for Mark III live on the **glasses** entries and apply **only** while **refit Mark III** glasses are **docked** in this implant—see `gear/` (files named `old-world-ar-tactical-glasses-mark-iii-*.md`).
