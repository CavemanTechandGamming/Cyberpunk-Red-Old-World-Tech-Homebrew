# Old World AR Charge Tether — Mark II Interface

**Short description:** A cranial implant that feeds **regulated power** to compatible **Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark II** while you wear them—**smarter** than the Mark I tether (thermal and charge protection), but **still not** a Mark III cranial dock: **no** high-bandwidth coupling, **no** hard neural link.

**Type:** Cyberware (implant)  
**Install:** Clinic or better (mastoid / temporal hardware; not street chop)  
**Cost:** **GM / table.** Set **eurodollars** to your economy and how this pairs with the glasses line.  
**Suggested price:** 500 eb — *baseline for balance; change freely to fit your campaign.*
**Humanity Cost:** 3 (or 1d6/3 rounded up if installed after character creation—GM choice)

## Description

The **Mark I Charge Tether** is a trickle line. The **Mark II** revision adds what salvage techies actually care about: a **charge governor**, **thermal shunts**, and **cell-conditioning logic** borrowed from pre-Collapse vehicle BMS modules—shrunk until it fits behind the ear.

When **Mark II** glasses are on your face and the coupling aligns, they draw clean power from your cyberware budget **without** cooking the old cells. The implant watches **pack temperature**, **charge rate**, and **voltage creep**; it **throttles** or **bleeds heat** before the HUD can trip a thermal fault. It can also **cut the tap** if something tries to **overcharge** the frame—cheap insurance when you are running a hot Agent stack all night.

You are still **not** docked like **Mark III**. There is **no** mechanical seat, **no** optical data spine, and **no** “legendary refit” bus—just power done **better** than Mark I, for glasses that are still **fully external**.

**Compatibility:** Intended for **Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark II** (generic, role-tuned, and alternate variants) with matching **charge pads** or a bundled **retrofit** at install (GM choice). **Not** compatible with **Mark III** dock-only frames (wrong architecture), and **not** a substitute for the **Mark III cranial dock**—those rigs do not use this workflow at all.

## While worn (Mark II + this implant)

- **Power & heat:** For **routine** play, ignore **daily / multi-day** battery tracking for **Mark II** glasses **while coupled**, and treat **overheat / overcharge** failures as **rare**—only when the GM stresses the scene (sabotage, cracked shunt, desert heat, damaged cells, or deliberate overclocking).  
- **Mark I glasses:** This tether **can** couple to **retrofitted Mark I** frames at the GM’s discretion, with **partial** benefit (cleaner charge than bare Mark I, but not the full Mark II thermal package unless the frames get the Mark II pad stack).

**Without glasses / wrong mark / coupling broken:** Low-profile chrome; no stat bonuses.

## Risks and limitations

- **No performance math:** Does **not** add Intelligence, Interface, attack bonuses, or NET boosts—those stay on the glasses (and on **Mark III** gear + dock).  
- **Theft and damage:** Glasses can still leave your face without your permission; the tether does not bolt them on.  
- **Still a headware attack surface:** EM, malware, and bad surgery apply like any mastoid implant.

## Custom Modifiers

**None** from this implant alone. It exists to **soften bookkeeping** and narrate **safer** charging for **Mark II**, not to stack modifiers on top of the glasses.
