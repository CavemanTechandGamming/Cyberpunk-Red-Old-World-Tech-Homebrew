# Old World AR Charge Tether — Mark I Interface

**Short description:** A small cranial implant that **only** feeds **power** to compatible **Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark I** while you wear them—no docking bus, no bandwidth upgrade, just juice.

**Type:** Cyberware (implant)  
**Install:** Clinic or Medtech with a decent kit (mastoid / temporal hardware; street chop at your table’s risk)  
**Cost:** 300 eb (homebrew; adjust to your campaign economy)  
**Humanity Cost:** 2 (or 1d6/3 rounded up if installed after character creation—GM choice)

## Description

The **Mark III cranial dock** is overkill if all you want is to stop babysitting Old World cells. The **Charge Tether** is the cheap, honest version: a thin **regulated power tap** and a **near-field coupling** patch (inductive or micro-contact—your ripperdoc’s preference) that lines up with the **temple arms** on compatible Mark I frames.

When the glasses are on your face and seated correctly, they **trickle-charge** from the same cyberware power budget that keeps the rest of your chrome alive. The glasses stay **external**—no neural pipe, no high-rate data spine—but their internal battery stops being the **primary** limiter during normal wear.

**Compatibility:** Works with **Old World AR Tactical Glasses — Mark I** (generic and role-tuned variants) **only if** the frames include the matching **charge pads** or factory loop (assume surviving Mark I units were built for this option, or assume a one-time **retrofit** bundled with the implant install—GM choice).

**Not compatible** with **Mark III** docking hardware (wrong coupling geometry), and it does **nothing** for random sunglasses.

## While worn (Mark I + this implant)

- **Power:** For **routine** play, you can **ignore daily / every-other-day battery tracking** for the Mark I glasses **while they are on your head and the tether is coupled**. The glasses still have cells for when you take them off.  
- **Stress:** The GM can still call for drain under **extreme** strain: Faraday cages, heavy EMP, damaged tether, blown power regulator, or running the HUD flat-out for hours in a heat crisis.

**Without glasses / wrong mark / coupling broken:** The implant is low-profile but visible if someone knows where to look. No bonuses—just a bit of chrome.

## Risks and limitations

- **No performance boost:** This does **not** add Intelligence, Interface, attack bonuses, or “snappier” NET. Those live on the glasses (and on the Mark III dock, if you go that route).  
- **Theft and damage:** Still true for the Mark I—someone can rip the glasses off your face; the tether does not hard-lock them to your skull.  
- **Another headware attack surface:** Malware, trauma, and bad installs happen—same family of problems as any small cranial implant.

## Custom Modifiers

**None** from this implant alone. It exists to **remove or soften the battery bookkeeping** for Mark I under normal conditions, not to stack more math on top of the glasses’ listed modifiers.
